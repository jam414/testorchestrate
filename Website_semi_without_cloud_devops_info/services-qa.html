Shift Left.



We will work with your team to identify the current testing practice and propose a viable solution in helping the team to shift left in their testing practice.


Overall, implementing shift left testing involves establishing a testing culture, identifying critical areas, defining test scenarios, implementing automation tools, integrating testing into the development process, and monitoring and measuring the effectiveness of the approach. The goal is to catch defects early and reduce the time and cost of testing.


Shift Left is a popular methodology used in software development that emphasizes the importance of testing early in the development cycle. In the context of QA, Shift Left means that testing activities should be initiated as early as possible in the development process, ideally at the beginning of the development cycle.
By adopting Shift Left in the QA process, teams can identify and address issues early, which can save time and money while improving the overall quality of the software. It also helps to ensure that requirements are clearly defined, that design decisions are well-informed and that defects are detected and fixed before they are baked into the final product.
One of the biggest benefits of Shift Left in the QA process is the reduction of testing time and costs. By identifying issues early in the development cycle, teams can avoid the need for costly and time-consuming rework, and can also reduce the time required for testing at the end of the development cycle.
Another benefit of Shift Left in the QA process is the improvement of collaboration between teams. By involving QA teams early in the development process, there is better alignment between development and testing activities, which can help to promote teamwork and a culture of collaboration.
Test Orchestrate can help QA teams to shift left effortlessly and help build high-quality software products that meet user needs, while also improving the efficiency and effectiveness of the development process. 

Continuous Testing.

We will help your team to adopt the the philosophy of fail fast. Deep Blue Automation team of experts will analyze your Quality process and help your organization to align people, process and technology and streamline your quality assurance process. 

We will work to identify a suitable automation tool and recommends the best practice to implement such a tool and if needed train the testing teams to adopt the tool.


Continuous testing is an essential practice for QA teams to help ensure that applications are high-quality and meet user needs. By continuously testing applications, QA teams can identify and address issues early in the development cycle, which can save time and money while improving the overall quality of the software.
In addition to traditional testing approaches, QA teams can also help developers adopt the "fail fast" philosophy, which emphasizes testing applications quickly and thoroughly to identify issues as early as possible. This approach can help developers address issues before they become bigger problems, and can also promote a culture of continuous improvement.
To support continuous testing and the fail-fast philosophy, QA teams can use a range of tools and techniques, including test automation, continuous integration, and continuous delivery. These practices can help teams identify issues quickly, ensure that applications are tested thoroughly, and streamline the overall development process.
By embracing continuous testing and the fail-fast philosophy, QA teams can help developers build high-quality software products that meet user needs, while also improving the efficiency and effectiveness of the development process.

Automate. Automate. Automate

Automation is a critical component of the QA process, enabling teams to test software quickly, efficiently, and accurately. Automation helps teams to streamline their testing process, reduce the time and effort required for testing, and increase the reliability of their testing results.
One of the key benefits of automation in the QA process is the ability to execute tests more quickly and efficiently. Automated tests can be run much faster than manual tests, and can be run repeatedly, ensuring consistent results. This allows teams to test more frequently, which can help to identify issues earlier in the development cycle, and can reduce the time required for testing overall.
Another benefit of automation in the QA process is increased accuracy and reliability. Automated tests are less prone to human error than manual tests, which can help to reduce the risk of false positives and false negatives. This can improve the overall quality of the software, ensuring that it meets user needs and functions as intended.
Finally, automation in the QA process can help teams to reduce costs and increase efficiency. Automated tests can be run 24/7, without the need for human intervention, which can save time and money. Additionally, by automating repetitive testing tasks, teams can free up their time to focus on more complex and creative testing activities.
Overall, automation is an essential component of the QA process, providing teams with the tools they need to test software quickly, accurately, and efficiently. By embracing automation in the QA process, teams can improve the quality of their software, reduce costs, and increase the speed and efficiency of their testing activities.

We will work with you to adopt a suitable automation strategy.

1. Assessment: The first step would be to assess the current state of the client's testing processes and identify areas that could be automated. This would involve reviewing existing test cases, processes, and tools used.

2. Tool Selection: Based on the assessment, suitable tools and frameworks for automation would be selected. The choice of tools would depend on the client's technology stack, testing requirements, and budget.

3. Test case selection: Not all test cases need to be automated, only the ones that are repetitive, time-consuming, and prone to human errors. Hence, a selection of test cases that are most suitable for automation will be identified.

4. Framework design: The next step would be to design a robust automation framework that can handle the selected test cases. The framework should be designed keeping in mind the scalability and maintenance of the automation suite.

5. Test script development: The test scripts would be developed using the chosen automation tool and framework. The scripts would be written in such a way that they are maintainable and can be updated easily.

6. Execution and reporting: Once the automation suite is ready, the test scripts would be executed on the identified test cases, and results would be recorded. The reporting mechanism would be designed in a way that it provides clear visibility of the test results and trends.

7. Maintenance: Finally, a maintenance plan would be put in place to ensure that the automation suite is kept up-to-date with any changes to the application or technology stack. This would involve regularly reviewing and updating the test scripts, fixing any issues, and enhancing the suite.

Overall, a QA automation implementation plan would involve careful planning, tool selection, test case selection, framework design, script development, execution, reporting, and maintenance. The goal is to create a scalable and maintainable automation suite that can provide quick feedback on the application's quality.
